# Demo Video Script (2:45 Maximum)

The final video must be public on YouTube, under three minutes, in English, and contain audio. Avoid third-party music.

## 0:00-0:15 - Title and Promise

**Visual:** Title screen; briefly switch between Chinese, English, and Bahasa Melayu, then select English.

**Narration:**

> The Last Label is a trilingual third-person heritage horror game built with Codex and GPT-5.6. You play a tourist whose museum guide begins rewriting the past around her.

## 0:15-0:42 - Front Hall

**Visual:** Walk through the front hall, show the over-the-shoulder camera, inspect an exhibit, and frame the Leong family photograph.

**Narration:**

> What begins as a normal visit to the fictional Leong House becomes an evidence-driven mystery. Each inspected object changes the tour, and people disappear from this 1919 family photograph.

## 0:42-1:08 - House and Interaction

**Visual:** Cross the courtyard in the rain, open a room door, walk up the staircase, and show the upper gallery.

**Narration:**

> I built a complete navigable house with functional doors, physical collision, a walkable upper gallery, and a compatibility-focused rain system that stays responsive on older integrated graphics.

## 1:08-1:35 - Puzzles

**Visual:** Show the beadwork motifs, then the kitchen recipe and banquet table.

**Narration:**

> The puzzles use domestic records as ciphers. Beadwork motifs hide an accounting trail, while an Ayam Pongteh recipe encodes the seating order from the family's final banquet.

## 1:35-1:58 - Evidence and Horror

**Visual:** Show the counting room, the abacus reading 1919, one brief shadow encounter, and the changed photograph.

**Narration:**

> Five linked clues reveal why Leong Yuecheng was erased from the archive. Horror comes from changing records, footsteps, empty rooms, and one short pursuit rather than constant combat.

## 1:58-2:22 - Ending

**Visual:** Place evidence in the ancestor hall and show the three ending choices, then select Truth.

**Narration:**

> The final exhibit depends on what the player preserved. They can restore the truth, accept a beautiful lie, or destroy the record and continue the cycle.

## 2:22-2:45 - Codex and GPT-5.6

**Visual:** Quick cuts of GDScript, automated test pass lines, and the running Windows build.

**Narration:**

> Codex with GPT-5.6 helped turn my story brief into the controller, multilingual state machine, modular scenes, performance fixes, and automated tests. I directed the narrative, cultural safeguards, puzzle meanings, and visual choices. This is a tested Windows build, not a mock-up.
